Kashmir Great Lakes Trek

Duration: 8 Days
Difficulty: Moderate to Challenging
Max Altitude: 13,800 ft
Best Season: Jul-Sep

Brief History

The KGL route was historically used by nomadic Bakarwal shepherds migrating with their flocks across the high passes. Over the last two decades, it has become one of India's most celebrated alpine trekking routes due to its extraordinary chain of glacier lakes. The trail connects ancient shepherd paths that have been used for centuries, offering trekkers a glimpse into the traditional pastoral lifestyle of Kashmir.

What It Is Famous For

  • Seven major alpine lakes including Vishansar, Krishansar, Gadsar, Satsar, and twin Gangbal-Nandkol
  • High mountain passes offering panoramic Himalayan views
  • Meadows stretching for miles with vibrant wildflowers
  • Considered the signature trek of Kashmir and one of India's top 5 treks
  • Diverse landscapes from pine forests to alpine meadows to rocky terrain

Day-by-Day Itinerary


Day 1: Arrive in Srinagar

Check-in at hotel, team briefing, equipment check, and trek preparation. Explore local markets if time permits.

Day 2: Srinagar → Sonamarg → Nichnai (11 km trek)

Drive to Sonamarg (2,800 m). Begin trek through forest trails transitioning to wide valleys. Camp at Nichnai meadows surrounded by towering peaks.

Day 3: Nichnai → Vishansar via Nichnai Pass (12 km)

Ascend to Nichnai Pass (4,080 m) with stunning views. Descend to the stunning Vishansar lake basin, camping beside the crystal-clear alpine lake.

Day 4: Explore Vishansar & Krishansar

Rest day for exploration and acclimatization. Visit nearby Krishansar lake, enjoy photography, and prepare for the challenging days ahead.

Day 5: Vishansar → Gadsar via Gadsar Pass (15 km)

Climb to the trek's highest point at Gadsar Pass (13,800 ft). Descend to the turquoise Gadsar lake, known as the "Lake of Flowers." Camp near the pristine waters.

Day 6: Gadsar → Satsar (12 km)

Cross flowering meadows and navigate several water crossings. Reach Satsar, a cluster of seven interconnected lakes. Camp in the valley.

Day 7: Satsar → Gangbal & Nandkol via Zaj Pass (9 km)

Cross Zaj Pass and reach the magnificent twin Gangbal and Nandkol lakes situated below the sacred Mount Harmukh (5,142 m).

Day 8: Gangbal → Naranag → Srinagar (13 km trek + drive)

Steep descent through pine forests to Naranag village. Drive back to Srinagar for final night stay and celebration.
