Kashmir Great Lakes Trek (KGL)

Maximum Altitude 13,800 ft (4,206 m)
Best Time July – September
Duration 8 Days
Difficulty Moderate to Challenging

Brief History

The KGL route was historically used by nomadic Bakarwal shepherds migrating with their flocks across the high passes. Over the last two decades, it has become one of India's most celebrated alpine trekking routes due to its extraordinary chain of glacier lakes. The trail connects ancient shepherd paths that have been used for centuries, offering trekkers a glimpse into the traditional pastoral lifestyle of Kashmir.

What It Is Famous For

  • Seven major alpine lakes including Vishansar, Krishansar, Gadsar, Satsar, and twin Gangbal-Nandkol
  • High mountain passes offering panoramic Himalayan views
  • Meadows stretching for miles with vibrant wildflowers
  • Considered the signature trek of Kashmir and one of India's top 5 treks
  • Diverse landscapes from pine forests to alpine meadows to rocky terrain

Detailed Itinerary

Day 1: Arrive in Srinagar

Check-in at hotel, team briefing, equipment check, and trek preparation. Explore local markets if time permits.

Day 2: Srinagar → Sonamarg → Nichnai (11 km trek)

Drive to Sonamarg (2,800 m). Begin trek through forest trails transitioning to wide valleys. Camp at Nichnai meadows surrounded by towering peaks.

Day 3: Nichnai → Vishansar via Nichnai Pass (12 km)

Ascend to Nichnai Pass (4,080 m) with stunning views. Descend to the stunning Vishansar lake basin, camping beside the crystal-clear alpine lake.

Day 4: Explore Vishansar & Krishansar

Rest day for exploration and acclimatization. Visit nearby Krishansar lake, enjoy photography, and prepare for the challenging days ahead.

Day 5: Vishansar → Gadsar via Gadsar Pass (15 km)

Climb to the trek's highest point at Gadsar Pass (13,800 ft). Descend to the turquoise Gadsar lake, known as the "Lake of Flowers." Camp near the pristine waters.

Day 6: Gadsar → Satsar (12 km)

Cross flowering meadows and navigate several water crossings. Reach Satsar, a cluster of seven interconnected lakes. Camp in the valley.

Day 7: Satsar → Gangbal & Nandkol via Zaj Pass (9 km)

Cross Zaj Pass and reach the magnificent twin Gangbal and Nandkol lakes situated below the sacred Mount Harmukh (5,142 m).

Day 8: Gangbal → Naranag → Srinagar (13 km trek + drive)

Steep descent through pine forests to Naranag village. Drive back to Srinagar for final night stay and celebration.

Tarsar Marsar Trek

Maximum Altitude 13,500 ft (4,115 m)
Best Time June – September
Duration 6 Days
Difficulty Moderate

Brief History

The Tarsar–Marsar region is tied deeply to Kashmir's ancient folklore and pastoral traditions. Shepherd communities have used these valleys for centuries during summer migrations. Marsar Lake holds mythological significance in local legends, believed to be a "mystic" lake always veiled by clouds. The trek gained popularity among adventure enthusiasts in the 1990s and is now considered a classic Kashmiri trek.

What It Is Famous For

  • Turquoise twin lakes - Tarsar and Marsar - surrounded by meadows
  • Gentle, scenic meadows perfect for beginners
  • Considered the most beautiful short trek of Kashmir
  • Rich biodiversity including Himalayan marmots and rare birds
  • Stunning views of Kolahoi Peak, the "Matterhorn of Kashmir"

Detailed Itinerary

Day 1: Arrive in Srinagar

Check-in, briefing, and preparation for the trek.

Day 2: Srinagar → Aru → Lidderwat (11 km trek)

Drive to Aru valley. Begin trek through dense pine forests along the Lidder River. Camp at Lidderwat meadows.

Day 3: Lidderwat → Shekwas (8 km)

Trek through wide grazing meadows, cross wooden bridges over mountain streams. Camp at Shekwas with views of surrounding peaks.

Day 4: Shekwas → Tarsar Lake (5 km)

Gradual ascent to reach the emerald-blue Tarsar Lake. Camp at the shore with spectacular mountain reflections.

Day 5: Tarsar → Sundersar → Marsar View → Homwas (10 km)

Cross the high pass, visit Sundersar lake, climb to the Marsar ridge for views of the mystical Marsar lake. Descend to Homwas for camping.

Day 6: Homwas → Aru → Srinagar

Descend to Aru, drive back to Srinagar.

Tulian Lake Trek

Maximum Altitude 12,800 ft (3,901 m)
Best Time June – October
Duration 4 Days
Difficulty Moderate to Challenging

Brief History

Tulian Lake sits in a dramatic amphitheater between the Zanskar and Pir Panjal ranges. Historically, it was a summer grazing region for local herders. The lake was discovered by trekkers in the early 20th century and later made famous for its bowl-shaped glacier setting. Despite its proximity to Pahalgam, it remains relatively less crowded, offering an authentic wilderness experience.

What It Is Famous For

  • A dramatic glacier amphitheatre with snow-capped walls
  • Steep & challenging ascent through varied terrain
  • Proximity to Pahalgam - perfect for a quick adventure
  • Baisaran meadows known as "Mini Switzerland of India"
  • Glacier-fed crystal clear lake surrounded by permanent snow

Detailed Itinerary

Day 1: Arrive in Srinagar

Check-in and preparation.

Day 2: Srinagar → Pahalgam → Baisaran → Tulian Valley (8 km trek)

Drive to Pahalgam. Trek through Baisaran meadows ("Mini Switzerland") with panoramic mountain views. Camp in Tulian Valley.

Day 3: Tulian Valley → Tulian Lake → Return to Camp (6 km)

Steep ascent to reach the glacier-fed lake surrounded by snow-capped walls. Explore the amphitheater and return to camp.

Day 4: Descend to Pahalgam → Srinagar

Descend through meadows, drive back to Srinagar.

Gangbal – Nandkol Trek (Naranag Route)

Maximum Altitude 12,000 ft (3,658 m)
Best Time June – October
Duration 4 Days
Difficulty Moderate

Brief History

Gangbal has been a sacred pilgrimage lake for Kashmiri Pandits for centuries, considered the abode of Lord Shiva. The trail through Naranag was once a vital trade path linking Kashmir with Harmukh's northern tribes. The ancient Naranag temple ruins at the base add historical significance to this spiritual trek.

What It Is Famous For

  • Twin alpine lakes - Gangbal and Nandkol - of spiritual significance
  • Stunning views of Mount Harmukh (5,142 m), the "Kailash of Kashmir"
  • Short yet rewarding trek perfect for beginners
  • Ancient Naranag temple complex dating back to 8th century
  • Crystal clear waters reflecting the majestic Harmukh peak

Detailed Itinerary

Day 1: Arrive in Srinagar

Check-in and trek preparation.

Day 2: Srinagar → Naranag → Trunkhol (10 km trek)

Drive to Naranag. Visit ancient temple ruins. Long forest climb through pine and fir forests into meadows. Camp at Trunkhol.

Day 3: Trunkhol → Gangbal & Nandkol (7 km)

Ascend to reach the beautiful twin lakes situated under the towering Harmukh peak. Camp beside the sacred waters.

Day 4: Gangbal → Naranag → Srinagar

Descend to Naranag, drive back to Srinagar.

Kousarnag Lake Trek

Maximum Altitude 13,000 ft (3,962 m)
Best Time July – September
Duration 5 Days
Difficulty Moderate

Brief History

One of Kashmir's largest glacier lakes, Kousarnag is mentioned in several ancient Hindu texts and local folklore. The lake has been visited by locals for centuries during seasonal migrations. The trek starts from the spectacular Aharbal waterfall, known as the "Niagara of Kashmir," adding to the trek's unique character.

What It Is Famous For

  • Massive glacier-fed lake - one of Kashmir's largest
  • Remote & untouched environment with minimal crowds
  • Trekking via Aharbal waterfall, the "Niagara of Kashmir"
  • Rich mythology and spiritual significance
  • Vast glacial basin surrounded by towering peaks

Detailed Itinerary

Day 1: Arrive in Srinagar

Check-in and preparation.

Day 2: Srinagar → Aharbal → Kongwattan (12 km trek)

Drive to Aharbal waterfall. Begin trek through forests and meadows. Camp at Kongwattan.

Day 3: Kongwattan → Kousarnag Lake (8 km)

Gradual ascent to reach the vast glacial basin and the magnificent Kousarnag lake. Camp beside the lake.

Day 4: Explore → Return to Kongwattan

Exploration day around the lake, then return to Kongwattan camp.

Day 5: Kongwattan → Aharbal → Srinagar

Descend to Aharbal, drive back to Srinagar.

Sheshnag Lake Trek

Maximum Altitude 11,700 ft (3,566 m)
Best Time June – September
Duration 3 Days
Difficulty Easy to Moderate

Brief History

Part of the historic Amarnath Yatra pilgrimage route, Sheshnag Lake derives its name from the mythical serpent god Sheshnag. The lake has been a spiritual rest point for pilgrims for thousands of years. According to legend, the lake is named after the seven-headed serpent that Lord Shiva wears around his neck.

What It Is Famous For

  • Religious and spiritual significance on the Amarnath Yatra route
  • Easy access from Pahalgam
  • Bright green alpine lake surrounded by snow peaks
  • Perfect for beginner trekkers and families
  • Well-established trail with good infrastructure

Detailed Itinerary

Day 1: Arrive in Srinagar

Check-in and preparation.

Day 2: Srinagar → Chandanwari → Pissu Top → Sheshnag Lake (14 km trek)

Drive to Chandanwari. Trek through scenic valleys, cross Pissu Top, and reach the beautiful Sheshnag lake. Camp beside the lake.

Day 3: Sheshnag → Chandanwari → Srinagar

Descend to Chandanwari, drive back to Srinagar.

Bandipora → Gangbal Trek (Arin/Kudara Route)

Maximum Altitude 11,800 ft (3,597 m)
Best Time July – September
Duration 5 Days
Difficulty Moderate

Brief History

This northern route to Gangbal was once used by Gujjar and Bakarwal tribes during their seasonal migrations. Only recently rediscovered by trekkers, it remains one of the most pristine offbeat trails in Kashmir. The route offers a unique perspective of the Harmukh massif from the north.

What It Is Famous For

  • Offbeat, crowd-free trail away from tourist circuits
  • Extraordinary views of Wular Lake - Asia's largest freshwater lake
  • Unique northern approach to Mount Harmukh
  • Interaction with nomadic Gujjar communities
  • Pristine wilderness and untouched landscapes

Detailed Itinerary

Day 1: Arrive in Srinagar

Check-in and preparation.

Day 2: Srinagar → Bandipora → Ridge Camp (10 km trek)

Drive to Bandipora. Begin trek through forests to ridge camp with views of Wular Lake.

Day 3: Ridge → Alpine Meadows → Harmukh North Base (12 km)

Trek through alpine meadows to reach Harmukh north base camp.

Day 4: Harmukh Base → Gangbal & Nandkol (8 km)

Reach the twin lakes from the northern approach. Camp beside Gangbal.

Day 5: Exit via Naranag → Srinagar

Descend via Naranag route, drive back to Srinagar.

Harmukh Circuit Trek (Ladakh Peak)

Maximum Altitude 16,000 ft (4,877 m)
Best Time July – September
Duration 6 Days
Difficulty Challenging

Brief History

Mount Harmukh (5,142 m), known as the "Kailash of Kashmir," has been revered for centuries in Hindu mythology. The route connecting Harmukh's ridge to the Ladakh-facing mountains was used by early explorers studying the climatic divide between Kashmir and Ladakh. This challenging trek offers a unique high-altitude experience.

What It Is Famous For

  • Ladakh-like landscapes with dramatic terrain changes
  • Panoramic ridge at 16,000 ft with 360-degree views
  • The legendary Mount Harmukh - sacred peak of Kashmir
  • High-altitude adventure for experienced trekkers
  • Transition from lush Kashmir valleys to barren Ladakh terrain

Detailed Itinerary

Day 1: Arrive in Srinagar

Check-in and preparation.

Day 2: Srinagar → Naranag → Trunkhol (10 km trek)

Drive to Naranag. Trek to Trunkhol for acclimatization.

Day 3: Trunkhol → Harmukh Base (8 km)

Ascend to Harmukh base camp with stunning views of the peak.

Day 4: Summit Ridge → Gangbal (12 km)

Early morning push to the summit ridge at 16,000 ft. Panoramic views of Kashmir and Ladakh ranges. Descend to Gangbal.

Day 5: Gangbal → Naranag (13 km)

Descend to Naranag village.

Day 6: Final night in Srinagar

Drive to Srinagar, rest and celebration.

Mahadev Peak Trek

Maximum Altitude 13,000 ft (3,962 m)
Best Time April – November
Duration 3 Days
Difficulty Moderate

Brief History

Mahadev Peak has served as a vantage point for shepherds and hunters for generations. Located close to Srinagar, it became a popular trek due to its exceptional accessibility and panoramic views. The peak offers one of the best 360-degree views of the Kashmir valley and surrounding Himalayan ranges.

What It Is Famous For

  • Best panoramic view over Srinagar and Dal Lake
  • Close proximity to the city - perfect for weekend treks
  • Popular sunrise and sunset trek destination
  • 360-degree views of Pir Panjal and Greater Himalayan ranges
  • Accessible almost year-round with proper preparation

Detailed Itinerary

Day 1: Arrive in Srinagar

Check-in and preparation.

Day 2: Srinagar → Dara → Astanmarg → Mahadev Base (12 km trek)

Drive to Dara. Trek through Astanmarg meadows to Mahadev base camp.

Day 3: Summit Mahadev Peak → Return to Srinagar (8 km trek)

Early morning summit push for sunrise views. Panoramic vistas of Kashmir valley. Descend and drive back to Srinagar.

Kolahoi Glacier Trek

Maximum Altitude 12,500 ft (3,810 m)
Best Time June – October
Duration 4 Days
Difficulty Moderate to Challenging

Brief History

Kolahoi Peak (5,425 m), often called the "Matterhorn of Kashmir," has drawn explorers since the British era. Treks to the glacier became famous in the 1930s when mountaineers first documented the region. The glacier feeds the Lidder River and is a major source of water for the Kashmir valley.

What It Is Famous For

  • Dramatic glacier viewpoint below the majestic Kolahoi Peak
  • Pahalgam's most iconic and challenging trek
  • Rugged terrain with boulders, moraines & ice formations
  • Source of the Lidder River
  • Stunning views of the "Matterhorn of Kashmir"

Detailed Itinerary

Day 1: Arrive in Srinagar

Check-in and preparation.

Day 2: Srinagar → Aru → Lidderwat (11 km trek)

Drive to Aru. Trek through pine forests to Lidderwat meadows.

Day 3: Lidderwat → Satlanjan → Kolahoi Glacier View → Return (14 km)

Long day trek through boulder fields and moraines to reach the glacier viewpoint. Spectacular views of Kolahoi Peak. Return to Lidderwat camp.

Day 4: Lidderwat → Aru → Srinagar

Descend to Aru, drive back to Srinagar.
